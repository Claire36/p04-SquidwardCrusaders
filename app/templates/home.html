Air pollutants pose a major risk to our health. They can penetrate our vital organs and cause diseases such as strokes, heart attacks, cancer, and more.

According to the University of Chicago's Energy Policy Institute, air pollution shortens life expectancy by 2.2 years on average worldwide.

Air pollutants are tiny (their diameters are a few hundred picometers), which lets them get past the body's natural defenses.

Air pollutants trap heat in the atmosphere, contributing to global warming.
